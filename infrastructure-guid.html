Japan Infrastructure Guide

Essential information for navigating foreign infrastructure

Capital: Tokyo Language: Japanese Currency: Japanese Yen (¥) Time Zone: JST (UTC+9)

Japan is an island country in East Asia. It is situated in the northwest Pacific Ocean and is bordered on the west by the Sea of Japan. Japan is a developed country known for its technological innovation, efficient public transportation, and unique blend of traditional and modern infrastructure.

Safety Assessment

Overall Safety Rating:
Very Safe

Crime Rate

Japan has one of the lowest crime rates in the world. Violent crime is rare, and theft is uncommon but can occur in crowded tourist areas.

Medical Facilities

High-quality medical care is widely available. Many hospitals in major cities have staff who speak English, but this is less common in rural areas.

Emergency Contacts

Police: 110
Ambulance/Fire: 119
Japan Helpline: 0570-000-911

Natural Disaster Awareness

Japan is prone to earthquakes, tsunamis, typhoons, and volcanic activity. Familiarize yourself with emergency procedures and download the Japan Meteorological Agency app for alerts.

Geography & Climate

Climate Zones

Japan's climate varies from cool temperate in the north to subtropical in the south. The country experiences four distinct seasons.

Spring (March-May)

Mild temperatures, cherry blossoms. Average: 10-20°C (50-68°F)

Summer (June-August)

Hot and humid. Average: 25-30°C (77-86°F)

Autumn (September-November)

Mild temperatures, autumn foliage. Average: 10-25°C (50-77°F)

Winter (December-February)

Cold with snow in northern regions. Average: 0-10°C (32-50°F)

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer the most pleasant weather for traveling throughout Japan.

Pre-departure Preparation

Visa Requirements

Many countries have visa exemption agreements with Japan allowing stays of up to 90 days. Check the Ministry of Foreign Affairs website for specific requirements for your nationality.

Power Outlets

Japan uses Type A and B sockets with 100V supply. Bring an adapter if your devices use different plugs.

Internet Connectivity

Options include pocket WiFi rental, SIM cards, or using free WiFi at hotels, cafes, and public spaces. Pre-booking pocket WiFi for airport pickup is recommended.

Transportation Cards

Consider purchasing a Suica or PASMO card for convenient travel on public transportation. These rechargeable IC cards can be used nationwide.

Arrival Guide

Airport to City Transportation

Express Trains

Narita Airport: Narita Express (N'EX) to Tokyo Station (approx. 60 min, ¥3,070)
Haneda Airport: Tokyo Monorail or Keikyu Line (approx. 30 min, ¥500-700)

Airport Limousine Bus

Direct service to major hotels and stations. More comfortable but slower than trains.
Cost: ¥1,000-3,000 depending on destination

Taxi

Convenient but expensive option.
Narita to Tokyo: Approx. ¥20,000-25,000
Haneda to Tokyo: Approx. ¥5,000-10,000

Essential Japanese Phrases

こんにちは (Konnichiwa)
Hello
ありがとう (Arigatou)
Thank you
すみません (Sumimasen)
Excuse me/I'm sorry
英語を話せますか? (Eigo o hanasemasu ka?)
Do you speak English?

First Day Tips

  • Purchase a Suica/PASMO card at the airport station
  • Withdraw cash from ATMs at 7-Eleven, post offices, or international ATMs
  • Pick up a pocket WiFi if pre-ordered
  • Consider activating your Japan Rail Pass if purchased
  • Convenience stores (konbini) are great for quick meals and essentials
