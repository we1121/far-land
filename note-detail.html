Popular European countries with a budget of just $10,000

John Traveler June 15, 2023 Europe, Budget Travel

Traveling through Europe has always been a dream for many, but the misconception that it requires a fortune often holds people back. I'm here to tell you that with careful planning and smart choices, you can explore multiple European countries with a budget of just $10,000.

Planning Your Route

The key to budget travel in Europe is planning an efficient route. I started my journey in Portugal, then made my way through Spain, France, Italy, and ended in Greece. By traveling in a logical sequence, I minimized transportation costs and maximized my time in each location.

Accommodation Hacks

Instead of expensive hotels, I opted for a mix of hostels, budget guesthouses, and occasional Airbnb stays. In some cities, I found hostels for as little as $20 per night, while private rooms in guesthouses averaged around $50-70. The best part? Many included breakfast, which saved me additional meal costs.

Transportation Tips

For long distances, I used a combination of budget airlines and trains. Booking flights well in advance saved me hundreds of dollars. Within cities, I either walked or used public transportation, which is excellent in most European cities. In some cases, I purchased city passes that included unlimited public transport and entry to major attractions.

Food and Dining

One of the joys of traveling through Europe is experiencing the diverse cuisines. To keep costs down, I ate breakfast at my accommodation when included, had picnic lunches with items from local markets, and splurged occasionally on dinner at local restaurants. Street food also proved to be both delicious and economical.

Must-Visit Budget-Friendly Destinations

While cities like Paris and Rome are incredible, they can be expensive. I found amazing value in places like Porto (Portugal), Valencia (Spain), Bologna (Italy), and Thessaloniki (Greece). These cities offer rich cultural experiences, stunning architecture, and delicious food at a fraction of the cost of their more famous counterparts.

With careful planning and smart choices, my three-month European adventure cost just under $10,000, including flights, accommodation, food, transportation, and activities. The memories and experiences I gained are priceless, proving that you don't need unlimited funds to explore the wonders of Europe.
